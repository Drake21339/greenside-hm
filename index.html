MARCH-PAWS Assessment

What is this?

MARCH is the life-threatening order: Massive hemorrhage, Airway, Respiration, Circulation, Hypothermia/Head. PAWS is stabilization for evacuation: Pain, Antibiotics, Wounds, Splinting. Order is critical.

How to use it: Tap Complete step on each letter. Select all and only the correct actions for that step to check it off.

    9-Line Medevac Request

    What is this?

    The 9-Line MEDEVAC is the US military / NATO standard for requesting medical evacuation (ref: TC 3-21.76, TCCC). Lines 1–5 launch the aircraft (transmit in ~25 sec); 6–9 can follow en route.

    How to use: Fill each line below (or use Load random scenario to pre-fill a practice case). Then tap Generate Summary to get the formatted 9-line block and the phonetic radio readout—practice reading that over the radio. For a timed drill under pressure, use the Radio Stress tab.
